# Daggerheart Quick Reference

## The Duality Dice
Roll **2d12** (Hope Die + Fear Die) + relevant Trait modifier.

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Beat difficulty, Hope die higher | **Success with Hope** — succeed + gain 1 Hope |
| Beat difficulty, Fear die higher | **Success with Fear** — succeed, but GM gains 1 Fear |
| Miss difficulty, Hope die higher | **Failure with Hope** — fail, but gain 1 Hope |
| Miss difficulty, Fear die higher | **Failure with Fear** — fail + GM gains 1 Fear |

> On a **Critical Success** (both dice match and beat the difficulty): something exceptional happens.

---

## The Six Traits
- **Agility** — speed, reflexes, dodging
- **Strength** — raw power, forcing, endurance
- **Finesse** — precision, dexterity, stealth
- **Instinct** — perception, survival, intuition
- **Presence** — persuasion, performance, leadership
- **Knowledge** — lore, magic, investigation

---

## Hope
- Gained: Success with Hope rolls, resting, certain abilities
- Spent by players to: activate abilities, add +1d6 to a roll, help allies, avoid consequences

## Fear
- Gained by GM: Fear die rolls, certain adversary abilities
- Spent by GM to: activate adversary special moves, introduce complications, trigger countdown clocks, make an NPC act dramatically

## Difficulty Thresholds
| Difficulty | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Easy | 10 |
| Standard | 15 |
| Hard | 20 |
| Formidable | 25 |

*The GM sets the difficulty before the roll is made.*

---

## Damage & Hit Points
- **Damage** is reduced by **Armor Score** first, then applied to HP
- **Major Damage Threshold:** damage at or above this value causes a Major Wound
- **Severe Damage Threshold:** damage at or above this value causes a Severe Wound
- At **0 HP:** mark a Death Move; if you have none left, your character dies or is taken out

---

## Stress
- Taken from: social attacks, magic, fear, certain adversary abilities, bad rolls in dangerous situations
- Cleared by: Short/Long Rests, certain abilities
- At max Stress: must clear Stress or begin marking it as HP damage

---

## Resting
**Short Rest:**
- Clear Stress (amount based on class)
- Use a Hit Die to recover HP (if available)
- Takes roughly 1 hour of in-fiction time

**Long Rest:**
- Clear all Stress
- Recover HP to full (or significant amount)
- Regain expended abilities/resources
- Takes a full night of safe rest
- May trigger Downtime Activities

---

## Countdown Clocks
- 4-segment or 6-segment clocks tracking escalating threats
- Advance when: the GM spends Fear, certain events happen, players fail rolls
- When full: the threat becomes real — consequences activate

---

## Adversary Types
| Type | Role |
|------|------|
| Minion | Weak, acts in groups, usually 1 attack per activation |
| Standard | Individual threat with special moves |
| Solo | Major villain or monster, multiple phases, high impact |

---

## Combat Turn Structure
1. **Players act** (can go in any order the group chooses)
2. **GM acts** (spends Fear to activate adversary moves, triggers environment)
3. **Repeat** until scene resolves

> The GM does not roll dice for adversaries — adversaries hit unless players take action to avoid, counter, or change the situation.

---

## Experience Tags
- Provide **+2** to any roll where they're narratively relevant
- Must be justified in fiction
- Examples: "Grew up at sea," "Former city guard," "Knows the old ways"

---

## Connections
- A PC-to-PC relationship established in character creation
- Can be activated (with player agreement) for narrative bonuses
- Should be updated as the campaign evolves
