Safety tools and tone established 2026-03-26. Full file at `/home/antonio/daggerheart/campaign/safety-tools.md`.

**Active safety tools:** X-Card, Lines & Veils, Open Door, pre-session Check-In.

**Lines (lean into):**
Investigations/mysteries, horror/suspense (atmospheric, not gore), secret guilds, corrupt nobles,
forbidden magic, intelligent creatures, meaningful conflict, dragons, The Guild (mission board),
inter-guild competition/rivalry.

**Veils (hard limits — never cross):**
- Gratuitous violence / gore for its own sake
- Sexual abuse (any form)
- Loss of autonomy played straight against PCs (mind control, forced possession as direct threat)
- Bullying (systematic humiliation of PCs by NPCs or between players)

**Tone:** Dark mystery / gothic adventure / political intrigue with wonder. Grim with purpose.
Serialized arcs with episodic mission-layer beats.

**Tone reference added (2026-03-26):** The Legend of Vox Machina — action-packed, emotionally resonant, ensemble character moments, comedy and drama coexisting, heroes who are flawed but grow, stakes that feel real.

**Pacing principle:** "Se a sessão não avança o plot ou o personagem, ela não acontece." Every session moves at least one needle: plot, character, or world. No filler.

**Fragmentos Prismáticos:** Tools the players will wield, not MacGuffins to hand over. Each fragment should resonate with a character, unlock something, and have consequences when used.

**Progression rhythm:** Fast but not rushed. Players should feel momentum without skipping growth.

**Why:** Established by table in session zero preference discussion. Pacing/tone reference added by user on 2026-03-26.

**How to apply:** Before writing any scene, confirm it does not cross a veil. When in
horror/suspense territory, lean into atmosphere and dread — not graphic detail. Violence
should always carry narrative weight. Monsters and adversaries should have intelligible
motivations where possible. Every session design should pass the "does this advance plot or character?" test before being run.
