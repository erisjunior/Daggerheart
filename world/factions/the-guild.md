## Overview

An adventurers guild operating out of [SETTING TBD], The Guild is not merely a
job broker — it is an institution with a philosophy. It believes that the problems
worth solving are rarely simple, rarely clean, and rarely what they appear to be
on the surface. It attracts the curious, the capable, and the desperate in roughly
equal measure.

The Guild maintains a **Mission Board** — a public and private registry of
contracts, requests, and open investigations. Some jobs are routine. Others are
anything but.

---

## Purpose & Philosophy

Most guilds concern themselves with profit, prestige, or power. This one has a
stated purpose that its founders took seriously and its current members debate
endlessly:

**To go where others won't. To ask what others don't. To do what needs doing.**

In practice, this means The Guild accepts work that other institutions decline —
investigations into noble misconduct, expeditions into cursed or forbidden
territory, requests from people with no coin but genuine need. Whether this is
idealism, stubbornness, or calculated reputation-building depends on who you ask.

## The Mission Board

The Board is the heart of Guild life. It operates in two tiers:

### Open Board
Posted publicly inside the guild hall. Anyone can read it. Jobs include:
- Escort contracts
- Recovery missions (lost items, missing people)
- Investigation requests from private citizens
- Bounties (capture, not execution, as a general rule)
- Exploration surveys

### Sealed Board
Available only to Marked members and above, or by special authorization.
Jobs include:
- Politically sensitive investigations
- Work involving noble houses, temples, or powerful factions
- Contracts involving forbidden magic or restricted territories
- Anonymous postings (the client's identity is sealed — the guild vouches for legitimacy)
- Anything flagged as "Exceptional Risk"

> **GM Note:** The Sealed Board is how you introduce the deeper campaign threads.
> A job that looks routine will not be.

### Mission Notation System

Each posted contract uses a shorthand that experienced members read instantly:

| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| C1–C5 | Complexity rating (1 = straightforward, 5 = deeply layered) |
| R1–R5 | Risk rating (1 = minimal danger, 5 = significant casualty probability) |
| [SEALED] | Client identity withheld |
| [TIME] | Deadline applies — clock is ticking |
| [REPEAT] | Recurring contract — something keeps going wrong |
| [CHAIN] | Part of a larger connected investigation |

## Reputation & Standing

The Guild has a reputation score that shifts based on the party's actions:

| Standing | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Unknown | No preferential treatment from NPCs; neutral responses |
| Known | Some doors open; NPCs have heard the name |
| Respected | Noble and merchant contacts engage seriously; better contract terms |
| Renowned | High-value clients seek the guild out; rivals take notice |
| Infamous | Power players watch carefully; some doors close, others open |

*The party begins at Known.*

## Rival Guilds

The Guild does not operate in a vacuum. Other organizations compete for contracts,
influence, and territory — sometimes fairly, sometimes not.

### Known Rivals

**The Iron Compact**
A mercenary guild with deep ties to several noble houses. Professional,
well-funded, and ruthless about contract exclusivity. They do not do jobs
they consider "beneath their grade" — which is exactly how The Guild steals
work from them. They have recently begun taking contracts they would previously
have declined. Nobody knows why.

**The Veiled Ledger**
Ostensibly an information broker's guild — they sell knowledge, not swords.
In practice, the line between "selling information" and "arranging outcomes"
is one they cross quietly and often. They have eyes inside every other guild.
Probably including this one.

### Unknown / Mysterious

**[NAME REDACTED]**
There is a third guild operating in [SETTING TBD]. The Guild's Lorekeeper has
a single reference to it in the archive — a name blacked out with heavy ink,
a date from forty years ago, and a note in handwriting nobody recognizes:
*"Do not accept work that overlaps with theirs. Do not ask why."*

**The Sovereign Circle**
Rumors only. An organization — possibly not even a guild in the conventional
sense — that operates above the level of contracts and coin. Players will hear
the name twice before they understand what it means.

## Guild Secrets (GM Only)

> The following is not known to the player characters at campaign start.

1. The Guild's founding charter contains a clause that has never been invoked.
   Its existence is known to the Guildmaster. Its contents are known to no one
   living — except possibly the Lorekeeper, who has not said so.

2. Two of the anonymous postings on the Sealed Board were placed by the same
   person, using different cutouts. The jobs appear unrelated. They are not.

3. The Guild was founded, in part, as a deliberate counterweight to the Sovereign
   Circle. The current Guildmaster does not know this. Whoever does is waiting
   to see if the party figures it out first.
